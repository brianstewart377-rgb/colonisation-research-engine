# EXP-0001 - A4 Complementary High Tech Role

## Purpose

Test whether A4 can serve as a complementary High Tech support body in Wregoe, where A5 already provides substantial Extraction, Refinery, and Industrial material coverage.

## Status

Awaiting completion and post-build evidence.

## Research question

Given that A5 already handles major Extraction/Refinery/Industrial coverage, does promoting High Tech to second place on A4 improve Wregoe's total construction-material coverage without unacceptable loss elsewhere?

## Not testing

This experiment is not testing whether Industrial + High Tech is universally better than Industrial + Extraction.

That comparison is invalid without system context. Viability depends on existing coverage, remaining slots, missing commodities, player goals, and opportunity cost.

## Hypothesis

If A4 becomes Industrial-dominant with High Tech as the second economy, then A4 may provide useful High Tech commodity coverage that complements A5's existing metals/refinery production.

## Prediction

- Industrial remains the dominant A4 economy.
- High Tech rises to approximately 250% and becomes second place.
- High Tech-related commodities become more visible or reliable.
- Wregoe's total material coverage broadens.
- Existing Industrial construction-material coverage remains acceptable.

## Context

- System: Wregoe colony project.
- Body: A4.
- Existing system role coverage:
  - A5: primary Extraction/Refinery/Industrial material producer.
  - A4: candidate complementary body.
- Player objective: maximise range of construction materials produced across the system.
- Player constraint: no planned cargo hauling.

## Before state

Known working baseline before 2 x High Tech Hubs:

- A4 economy: Industrial dominant.
- A4 secondary economy: Extraction.
- A4 High Tech coverage: approximately 10%.
- A4 should not be treated as dedicated High Tech coverage before this experiment.

- High Tech becomes second but expected commodities do not appear.

- Economy label or commodity behaviour does not match the projected percentage change.
