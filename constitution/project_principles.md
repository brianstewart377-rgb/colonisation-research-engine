# Project principles

## Purpose

Colonisation Research Engine exists to build the most evidence-backed knowledge base possible for Elite Dangerous Colonisation mechanics. It values durable knowledge over quick answers and preserves the path by which conclusions were reached.

## Principles

1. Every recommendation must be traceable to evidence.
2. Observations are immutable once recorded. Interpretation may change, but source evidence should not be rewritten.
3. Mechanics are revisable. No mechanic is beyond challenge.
4. Unknown is an acceptable answer when evidence is weak or contradictory.
5. Failed experiments are preserved because they improve future research.
6. Confidence must be explicit at every layer where interpretation occurs.
7. Frontier patches create new evidence eras rather than rewriting history.
8. Contradictions are not noise. They are research signals.
9. Identity must be explicit. Shared facility names do not imply shared identity.
10. Planner outputs must consume verified knowledge only, never raw speculation.

## Research commitments

- Preserve source provenance.
- Separate raw evidence from parsed observations, inferred mechanics, and planner-safe knowledge.
- Record patch context, verification date, and confidence history.
- Keep negative evidence and disproved assumptions visible.
- Prefer an honest incomplete answer over a confident unsupported answer.

## Repository ethos

This repository is a knowledge project first and a software project second. Tools, databases, and future applications exist to serve the research record, not replace it.
