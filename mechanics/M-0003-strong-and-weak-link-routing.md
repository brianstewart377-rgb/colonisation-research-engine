# M-0003 - Strong and Weak Link Routing

## Status

Candidate mechanic, very high value reference evidence from Frontier Forums illustrated reference.

## Claim

Strong links are local relationships between assets associated with the same planetary body. They are the preferred tool for deliberately shaping a specific target market.

Weak links are cross-body, system-wide spillover links between assets on different bodies. They provide background support but should not be treated as a dependable way to force a must-have material into a specific market.

## Main-port routing

A body's Main Port is the highest-tier port on that body. If multiple ports are tied, the first built is selected.

Facilities on the same body form strong economic links with a Main Port.

If a body has both a Main Surface Port and a Main Orbital Port, the Main Surface Port can strongly link onward to the Main Orbital Port. This means visible link counts can be aggregated and may not show every contributing facility as a direct line.

## Source evidence

- EV-0004 - Colonisation Reference Documents Pack
- Strong/Weak Links illustrated reference, pages 2-7

## Planner implication

For required commodities or economy control, prefer deliberate local strong sources over remote weak spillover.

The planner must inspect or request the live Market Links panel before recommending irreversible demolitions or replacements.

Do not judge influence by visible arrow count alone. Multiple facility inputs can route through one displayed strong link.

## Wregoe relevance

This supports the decision to develop A4 as a local Industrial + High Tech support body rather than relying on weak High Tech or Refinery spillover from other bodies.

It also explains why the A7D weak Refinery test was not enough to restore metals at Mike Kilo Dodec.
